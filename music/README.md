# Background Music

Drop royalty-free `.mp3`, `.m4a`, `.wav`, `.ogg`, or `.aac` files into this
folder and the pipeline will randomly pick one per video, mix it under the
speech at -18dB with a 1.5s fade in/out.

If the folder is empty, videos are produced without music — no breakage.

## Where to get free tracks

All of these are usable on monetized YouTube content:

- **YouTube Audio Library** — https://www.youtube.com/audiolibrary
  Sign in to your channel, filter by mood (Calm / Inspirational), genre
  (Cinematic / Ambient / Latin / Acoustic), and "No attribution required".

- **Pixabay Music** — https://pixabay.com/music/
  Browse by tag (lofi, ambient, latin, spanish guitar). Free for commercial
  use, no attribution required.

- **Free Music Archive** — https://freemusicarchive.org/
  Filter by license: prefer "CC0" or "CC BY" tracks.

- **Uppbeat** — https://uppbeat.io/  (free tier with limits)

## Recommended vibe for vocabulary Shorts

- Lo-fi hip-hop (relaxed study vibe — high retention for educational shorts)
- Ambient acoustic / classical guitar (Latin atmosphere without overpowering)
- Soft cinematic synths (modern feel)

Avoid tracks with vocals — they fight the narration.

## How many tracks?

Start with 5-10 different tracks. Random selection per video keeps the
channel feeling fresh; viewers won't recognize the same loop on every short.

## File naming

Doesn't matter — the picker globs the whole directory. Keep file names
descriptive for your own sanity (e.g. `lofi_calm_01.mp3`,
`acoustic_guitar.mp3`).
